Welcome to What Rock Ya Got! This website is all about trying to identify rocks you do have, with the knowledge that you don't have. Below is a handy little questionnare that should aid you in discovering your geological finds.

Let's get started! Begin by identifying which of the three rock types your rock is, then answer the following questions.

Igneous Rock

The igneous rock is formed as a result of volcanic processes. Due to this, the minerals of the rock are often crystallized and fused together with no surrounding cement. Commonly found near volcanos and underground.




Click here if rock is Igneous

Sedimentary Rock

Sedimentary rock is formed as a result of deposits. Due to this, the rocks appear layered, often weathered. The particles align themselves horizontal to the pressure.Commonly found near rivers, oceans, and in deserts. (Often contain fossils!)

Click here if rock is Sedimentary

Metamorphic Rock

Metamorphic rock is formed as a result of changes in heat and pressure. Due to this, they often appear twisted, folded, or swirled as though altered. Commonly found in mountanous regions.

Igneous rock identification tips

  • Tough
  • Little texture or layering
  • Contain black, white, and/or gray minerals

Sedimentary rock identification tips

  • Hardened sediment
  • Sandy, clay-like layers
  • Brown to gray in color, wind or water marks

Metamorphic rock identification tips

  • Tough, with straight or curved layers
  • Composed of light and dark minerals
  • Various colors, often contains glittery mica
